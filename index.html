I am a MSE student and teaching assistant at at Princeton University, where my research interests broadly span machine learning (ML) and are currently focused on graph ML.

At Princeton, I am currently a member of the lab Vertaix, working on developing a universal SE(3) invariant generative graph model for small molecules in 3D, as well as developing a method to directly regularize any model for diversity by optimizing Vendi score.

I received a B.S. in both Computer Science and Mathematics from Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology in May 2022, and have completed software engineering internships at Amazon Web Services, Kratos Defense & Security Solutions, and Collins Aerospace, and a research internship at Circa.

While at Rose-Hulman, I received many awards, including the Herman A. Moench Distinguished Senior Commendation, Frank Young Outstanding Service Award, and Henry Turner Eddy Award for Applied Mathematics, among others.

Spectral Touching Points in Two-Dimensional Materials
Andrea Wynn
SIURO, 2022

I find and prove the existence of Dirac conical points in various 2D materials under certain conditions on electric potential, a property which has been conjectured to be related to the unique properties of graphene. I additionally discovered and proved the existence of a new type of spectral touching point, which I named the mesa touching point.
